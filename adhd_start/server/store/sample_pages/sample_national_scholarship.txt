PROGRAM: National Scholarship — Sample (based on U of T structure)

OVERVIEW
The National Scholarship recognizes students who combine outstanding academic achievement with original and creative thinking and strong community or school leadership. Nomination by a Canadian secondary school (one nominee per school, typically via a “National Book Award” process) is required before applying.

ELIGIBILITY (summary)
- Citizenship/Status: Canadian citizen, permanent resident, or protected person.
- School standing: Final year of Canadian secondary school or first‑year CEGEP.
- Admission: Must apply to at least one undergraduate program at the host university for the same academic year (or plan a one‑year deferral).
- Nomination: Must be nominated by your school for the National Book Award.

APPLICATION REQUIREMENTS
- Online scholarship application (access typically follows submission of the university admission application).
- One confidential reference letter from a teacher, counsellor, or community leader (sent directly by the referee).
- Short‑answer responses and a longer statement demonstrating originality, creativity, and community/leadership impact.
- Official/unofficial transcripts as requested; additional documents may be required for verification.

SELECTION CRITERIA (typical themes)
- Original & creative thinking.
- High academic achievement.
- Evidence of leadership and contribution to school/community.
- Personal initiative and integrity.

IMPORTANT DATES & DEADLINES (sample, 2025 cycle style)
- School nomination deadline (National Book Award): 2025‑10‑10 23:59 ET
- Student deadline to apply to the university: 2025‑10‑17 23:59 ET
- Scholarship application deadline: 2025‑10‑31 23:59 ET
- Referee letter due: 2025‑11‑07 23:59 ET
- Finalist interviews: 2026‑02 (virtual; selected candidates only)

AWARD VALUE (illustrative)
- Up to four years of support for tuition, incidental, and residence fees at the host university.
- Runners‑up may receive named entrance awards with fixed annual values.

AI POLICY (explicit)
Use of generative AI tools (e.g., “ChatGPT” or similar) to author or compose any portion of the undergraduate award application is **not permitted**. Submissions containing AI‑generated content may result in **disqualification** from award eligibility. Applicants should produce their own original work and follow institutional academic integrity expectations.

FRAUD & INTEGRITY REMINDERS
- Do not pay third‑party “agents” who claim to secure awards or write applications.
- Provide accurate information; falsification can lead to disqualification and discipline.

CONTACT / SUPPORT (example)
- Scholarship inquiry web form (for nominees, referees, and counsellors).
- Guidance for school officials on nominating a single candidate per school year.
